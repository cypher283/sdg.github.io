Everything you need to know about the Sustainable Development Goals

What are the Sustainable Development Goals? +

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are 17 interconnected global goals adopted by all United Nations Member States in 2015. They serve as a universal call to action to end poverty, protect the planet, and ensure that by 2030 all people enjoy peace and prosperity.

When were the SDGs established? +

The SDGs were adopted by the United Nations General Assembly on September 25, 2015, as part of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development. They officially came into effect on January 1, 2016.

How can I contribute to achieving the SDGs? +

Everyone can contribute! Start by reducing waste, conserving water and energy, supporting local businesses, volunteering in your community, educating others about sustainability, and making conscious consumer choices. Complete our daily challenges to track your impact!

Why are there 17 goals? +

The 17 goals were developed through extensive consultation with governments, civil society, and other stakeholders worldwide. Each goal addresses a critical area of sustainable development, from poverty and hunger to climate action and partnerships. They are interconnected, meaning progress in one area affects progress in others.

What is the deadline for achieving the SDGs? +

The target year for achieving the SDGs is 2030. This gives countries and organizations 15 years from the goals' adoption to make significant progress toward sustainable development worldwide.

How is progress measured? +

Progress is measured through 169 targets and over 230 indicators. Countries report their progress annually, and data is compiled by the UN Statistics Division. Various organizations also track progress through reports and data visualization tools.

Are the SDGs legally binding? +

No, the SDGs are not legally binding. However, countries are expected to take ownership and establish national frameworks for achieving the goals. Governments decide how to track progress and incorporate the SDGs into their national planning processes.

How do the SDGs relate to climate change? +

Climate change is directly addressed in Goal 13 (Climate Action), but it affects almost all other SDGs. Climate impacts threaten food security, water availability, health, economic growth, and more. Addressing climate change is essential for achieving sustainable development.
